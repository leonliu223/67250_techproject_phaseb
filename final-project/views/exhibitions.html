Current Exhibitions

Explore our rotating exhibitions featuring international artists working at the intersection of art and technology.

Echoes in the Machine

On view through June 2026

Echoes in the Machine is a large-scale immersive installation that explores the relationship between human emotion and artificial intelligence. Visitors walk through a responsive environment where generative algorithms translate movement and sound into evolving visual landscapes. The exhibition asks a provocative question: can a machine learn to feel? Featuring works by five international artists working at the intersection of neural networks and sculptural media, this exhibition challenges assumptions about creativity, empathy, and what it means to be alive in a digital age.

Pixel & Pigment: A History of Digital Color

On view through September 2026

From the early days of cathode-ray monitors to today’s HDR displays and computational photography, color has been central to how we process and share visual information. Pixel & Pigment traces the surprising history of digital color theory through archival materials, interactive demos, and original artworks. Visitors can experiment with color mixing algorithms, explore the evolution of screen technology, and see how artists have used digital tools to push the boundaries of chromatic expression across five decades.

Unstructured Data

Opening April 2026

Unstructured Data is a group exhibition featuring eight emerging artists who use raw data — sensor readings, social media streams, satellite imagery, and financial records — as their primary artistic medium. By transforming information into sound, sculpture, and moving image, these artists reveal hidden patterns in the world around us. The exhibition invites visitors to reconsider the aesthetics of information overload and the quiet beauty embedded in everyday data.
